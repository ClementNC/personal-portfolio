## The Stable Matching Problem

Given $n$ men and $n$ women, each with a strict preference ordering over all members of the opposite group, find a stable matching — a perfect matching with no blocking pairs.

A **blocking pair** $(m, w)$ exists when:

- $m$ prefers $w$ over his current partner, and
- $w$ prefers $m$ over her current partner.

## Gale-Shapley Algorithm

```
Initialize all men and women as free
while some man m is free and hasn't proposed to every woman:
  w = highest-ranked woman m hasn't proposed to
  if w is free:
    (m, w) become engaged
  else if w prefers m over current partner m':
    m' becomes free
    (m, w) become engaged
  else:
    w rejects m
```

**Claim:** Gale-Shapley always terminates and produces a stable matching.
